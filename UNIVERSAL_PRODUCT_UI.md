# Universal Product UI

## 1. Product principle

One product, many coffee shops.

- one shared UI/UX;
- many workspaces;
- no per-client custom interface by default.

The product should feel like a reusable staff tool that a cafe joins, not like a new app design for every customer.

## 2. What can be customized

Limited customization is allowed at the workspace level:

- workspace name;
- logo / avatar;
- locations / spots;
- users;
- roles;
- recipes;
- checklists;
- menu / items content.

## 3. What is not customized

The following should stay consistent across workspaces:

- interface structure;
- main navigation;
- components;
- design system;
- forms;
- cards;
- workflow logic.

## 4. Visual direction

The UI should be:

- neutral and premium;
- suitable for coffee and hospitality teams;
- mobile-first;
- Telegram Mini App friendly;
- calm and readable;
- warm in background tone;
- accented with dark olive or coffee colors;
- typography-first, not brand-heavy;
- free of per-client theme reinvention at MVP stage.

## 5. How to use TRUD

TRUD is the first pilot workspace.

Use TRUD for:

- real pilot content;
- demo data;
- video instructions;
- examples of recipes and checklists;
- validating workflows with a real team.

TRUD is not the visual identity of the whole product.

## 6. Future customization

Later, when the product is mature, it may support limited customization such as:

- workspace logo;
- limited accent color;
- light/dark mode;
- simple theme presets.

That is future scope only.
